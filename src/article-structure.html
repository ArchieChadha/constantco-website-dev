Company vs trust: questions to ask first

8 November 2025 · 6 min read

Choosing a business structure is rarely “just a tax decision.” Companies, trusts, partnerships and sole traders each carry different compliance costs, flexibility for admitting investors, and implications for asset protection and estate planning.

Tax and profit distribution

Companies pay tax at the corporate rate (with nuances for base rate entities and other rules). Trusts generally flow income to beneficiaries, who are taxed in their own hands — but trust law and streaming rules add complexity. What looks efficient this year may not suit a sale or succession event in five years.

Asset protection and liability

Limited liability in a company can ring-fence trading risk, but personal guarantees and director duties still matter. Trusts can separate legal and beneficial ownership, yet lenders and regulators will look through to underlying controllers. Legal advice is essential alongside tax advice.

Reporting and administration

Consider ongoing ASIC fees, financial reporting thresholds, trust deed updates and minutes. A structure that saves tax but drowns you in paperwork may not be sustainable.

Questions for your advisers

  • Who might own or invest in the business in the next decade?
  • Will you hold appreciating assets (property, IP) inside the same entity that trades?
  • How would you exit — trade sale, management buy-out, or intergenerational transfer?
