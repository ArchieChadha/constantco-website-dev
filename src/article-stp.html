STP Phase 2: staying compliant

10 January 2026 · 4 min read

Single Touch Payroll Phase 2 expanded the detail reported with each pay run — including income types, country codes and more granular leave and allowance information. The goal is fewer gaps between employer reports and Services Australia, and clearer data for employees.

Why pay codes matter

Your payroll system should map earnings, deductions and allowances to the correct STP labels. Misclassified amounts can flow through to tax estimates, payment summaries and government benefits — often noticed only when something does not reconcile at year-end.

Leave and other entitlements

Annual leave, personal leave and other leave types need to be reported consistently with your award or agreement and your software’s STP settings. If you change payroll products mid-year, plan a migration checklist with your bookkeeper so opening balances and YTD figures stay intact.

Practical habits

  • Reconcile each pay run before finalising STP submissions.
  • Document any one-off payments (bonuses, termination pays) with clear reasons.
  • Review employee profiles when someone changes visa status, residency or working hours.
