GST for contractors: what to watch

20 February 2026 · 5 min read

Contractors often move between sole trader and company structures, cross GST thresholds mid-year, or work through labour-hire arrangements. Getting GST right on invoices and activity statements keeps cash flow predictable and reduces ATO queries.

Registration thresholds

If your annual turnover meets or is likely to meet the GST registration threshold, you generally need to register for GST, charge GST on taxable supplies, and lodge BAS (or report via other arrangements if applicable). Ceasing to be registered has its own timing and adjustment rules.

What invoices should show

Tax invoices for GST must include required particulars: your ABN, the buyer’s identity, what was sold, the GST-inclusive price or the GST amount, and the extent to which each sale is taxable. Poor invoices make it harder for clients to claim credits and for you to defend your own records.

Common BAS mistakes

  • Claiming GST on expenses that are GST-free or input-taxed without checking the rules.
  • Mismatch between income declared in BAS and what appears in your tax return.
  • Ignoring adjustments when payment dates differ from invoice dates (cash vs accruals).

If your income jumps part-way through the year, speak with your adviser about registration timing and whether any contracts should be reviewed.
