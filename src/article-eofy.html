EOFY checklist for small businesses

15 March 2026 · 4 min read

The weeks before 30 June are when small-business owners and bookkeepers line up records, close loose ends and make sure nothing important is missed before the year rolls over. This checklist is general in nature — your adviser can help you apply it to your entity and industry.

Reconcile BAS and bank feeds

Match every transaction in your accounting file to bank statements. Catching duplicates, missing invoices and miscoded GST now saves painful amendments later. If you report GST on a cash basis, confirm payment dates align with what you have claimed.

Superannuation and payroll

Check that super guarantee payments for employees were made on time and that your payroll categories (including any salary sacrifice) are mapped correctly for STP. Outstanding super can attract penalties and is not deductible until paid.

Chart of accounts and fixed assets

Review your chart for accounts you no longer use, and ensure depreciation and immediate write-off rules for eligible assets are reflected consistently. If you disposed of equipment or vehicles, document dates and proceeds for your tax workpapers.

Deductions and prepayments

Identify expenses you can bring forward or that relate specifically to the current year. Rules around prepayments, entertainment and motor vehicles differ — do not assume a blanket approach.

  • Export key reports (P&L, balance sheet, aged receivables/payables) for your records.
  • Back up your file and store a copy off-site or in the cloud.
  • Book a meeting with your accountant while there is still time to act on their suggestions.
