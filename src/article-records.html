Record keeping that survives an ATO review

5 August 2025 · 4 min read

Good records are not about hoarding paper — they are about being able to reconstruct what happened, why a deduction was claimed, and how GST was treated, often years after the fact. The ATO’s systems increasingly match data from banks, employers and platforms; gaps in your story invite questions.

Digital receipts and bank feeds

Cloud accounting with bank feeds saves time, but you still need source documents for material expenses. Match each feed transaction to an invoice or receipt, and add a short note if the description in the bank line is vague.

Naming and folders

A simple scheme beats chaos: YYYY-MM_vendor_purpose for scanned files, and consistent account codes in your ledger. When your bookkeeper or auditor asks for “all Q2 marketing spend,” you should not be searching three inboxes and a shoebox.

How long to keep records

General requirements depend on your situation (individual vs entity, amendments, capital gains assets). Your adviser can confirm retention periods — especially for assets held many years or where losses are carried forward.

  • Reconcile at least monthly; do not leave a full year to the last week of June.
  • Separate business and personal cards where possible.
  • Export and back up your accounting file after major year-end adjustments.
